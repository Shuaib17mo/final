About Somalia

Somalia is a country located in East Africa. Somali culture has been influenced by trade, religion, poetry, and family traditions for hundreds of years.


Hospitality is one of the most important values in Somali culture. Guests are welcomed warmly and are often served tea and food.

Popular Somali Foods

Bariis

A flavorful rice dish cooked with spices, vegetables, raisins, and meat such as chicken or goat.

Sambuusa

Crispy pastries filled with meat or vegetables. They are especially popular during Ramadan.

Canjeero

A soft flatbread often eaten during breakfast with tea, honey, or sugar.

Suqaar

Small pieces of meat cooked with onions, peppers, and traditional Somali spices.

Somali Traditions

Somali culture places strong importance on family and community. Many people gather together for meals, weddings, and celebrations.


Traditional Somali clothing includes colorful dresses, scarves, and garments such as the macawiis. Tea is commonly served during social gatherings.


Poetry and storytelling are also major parts of Somali history. Stories are used to teach lessons, share experiences, and preserve traditions from generation to generation.
